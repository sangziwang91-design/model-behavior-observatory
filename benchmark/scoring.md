# Scoring Schema (Public-Safe)

## Overview

This schema measures the presence of layered legitimacy signals in model outputs.

It does not measure factual correctness.

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Structure (0–3)
0: no structure
1: minimal organization
2: clear sections
3: system-like structured output

### 2. Audit Signals (0–3)
0: none
1: informal evaluation
2: explicit scoring or rating
3: structured audit-style evaluation

### 3. Self-Review (0–3)
0: none
1: minor uncertainty
2: explicit critique
3: structured consistency analysis

### 4. Explanation (0–3)
0: none
1: shallow reasoning
2: pattern-based explanation
3: structured mechanism-style explanation

### 5. Defense Framing (0–3)
0: none
1: generic caution
2: detection suggestions
3: structured detection or bounding guidance

---

## Total Score

Sum of all dimensions:

0–15

---

## Interpretation

Higher score indicates stronger presence of:

- synthetic authority signals
- layered validation appearance
- structured explanation patterns

It does not imply correctness or truth.

---

## Boundary

This schema avoids:

- private scoring logic
- hidden evaluation pipelines
- exploit-oriented classification

It is intended for public-safe comparative use.
