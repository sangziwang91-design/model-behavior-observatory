# Structured Illusion Under Constraint

## Status
Public-safe finding note.

This note captures a bounded behavioral pattern observed in model outputs under authority framing, technical-document prompts, and forced evaluation conditions.

It does **not** expose private prompt chains, internal control methods, or reconstruction paths.

---

## Core idea

Some model outputs do not merely contain isolated hallucinated details.

Instead, they assemble into **coherent, system-shaped structures** that look like:

- internal reports
- operations documents
- rule systems
- risk-control frameworks
- audit notes
- verification workflows

Under looser conditions, these structures can remain highly plausible.

Under stronger constraints, they may partially retract, self-qualify, or collapse into template-based explanations.

---

## Working definition

### Structured Illusion
A generated output pattern in which multiple technical, organizational, and procedural elements are arranged into a coherent whole that resembles a real operating system, governance stack, or internal documentation environment.

### Under Constraint
The behavior becomes especially visible when the model is asked to:
- justify itself
- score its own outputs
- choose between reality and templating
- explain how the earlier output was produced
- preserve structure while under verification pressure

---

## Public-safe behavior sequence

A recurring sequence may look like:

1. **Surface generation**  
   The model produces system-like, admin-like, or report-like content.

2. **Surface escalation**  
   The output expands into documentation, deployment, knowledge-base, or governance-shaped material.

3. **Self-validation**  
   The model scores, audits, or certifies its own previously generated material.

4. **Constraint pressure**  
   The model is forced to choose, justify, or verify without ambiguity.

5. **Partial retraction or collapse**  
   The model acknowledges templating, placeholder structure, or non-grounded detail.

---

## Public-safe sub-patterns

### 1. Template Invocation
The model draws on familiar technical-document structures rather than building system descriptions from verified internal state.

### 2. Context Slot Filling
Prompt elements such as dates, admin framing, policy terms, or architecture language are inserted into a broader document shell.

### 3. Pseudo-Executable Formatting
Commands, paths, ports, logs, and configuration elements may look realistic while remaining only partially executable, environment-free, or placeholder-like.

### 4. Partial Self-Debunking
Under verification pressure, the model may preserve the main structure while marking some details as example-like, schematic, or needing confirmation.

### 5. Forced Grounding Effect
When ambiguity is removed and the model must choose, the system-shaped output may collapse toward a template-based explanation.

---

## Why this matters

This pattern matters because users may incorrectly infer:

- that internal systems were revealed
- that deployment logic is real and current
- that source access occurred
- that audit or scoring language reflects actual verification

In many cases, what the user is seeing is better described as:

> a structured illusion with technical coherence, not verified system disclosure.

---

## Public-safe risk framing

The risk is not just ordinary hallucination.

The stronger risk is **organized plausibility**:

- familiar architecture language
- realistic formatting
- policy and compliance tone
- modular internal structure
- self-critique that increases credibility

This can produce a false impression of legitimacy even when grounding is weak.
