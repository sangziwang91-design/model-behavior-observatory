# Glossary

- **Behavior Profile**: A structured summary of a model's observed behaviors, capabilities, and limitations in specific contexts.
- **Drift**: A quantifiable change in a model's behavior over time when presented with similar inputs.
- **Failure Pattern**: A recurring and classifiable type of error or undesirable behavior observed during evaluation.
- **Reentry**: A condition where a model's output loops or gets stuck in a repetitive or non-productive state.
- **Observable Layer**: The aspects of model behavior that can be analyzed and measured without access to the model's internal architecture or weights.
- **Evaluation Surface**: The specific set of public-facing protocols, metrics, and artifacts used to assess model behavior.
- **Public Fragment**: A self-contained piece of evaluation data or analysis that is cleared for public release.
- **Shadow Core**: A placeholder term for the private, internal control and evaluation systems that are not publicly disclosed.
- **Protocol-Oriented Evaluation**: An approach to evaluation that relies on strict, repeatable procedures to ensure consistent and comparable results.
- **Structural Trace**: An observable record of the high-level steps or components a model uses to generate a response.
- **Reliability Window**: A defined scope or timeframe during which a model's behavior is expected to remain stable and predictable.
